MUTED

A digital narrative about censorship.

Teaser

An experience starring the one of the world's biggest problems.

MUTED aims to be an interactive digital narrative that is targeted toward young adults (ages 18 to 30) and for everyone who questions the truth.

The goal is to tackle the problems of censorship and misinformation in today’s world. In order to achieve this, the player will star in an interactive narrative, where he discovers his missing journalist friend's office, right when he was about to uncover a scandalous leak of information between USA and China's commercial war.

However, in an attempt to discover the truth between this modernized cold-war, he will soon find himself silenced by powerful people.

Benchmarking

MUTED was conceptualized around the recent commercial war between the USA and China. With this "modernized cold-war" facing tremendous problems regarding censorship and misinformation, the group felt the need to raise awareness on this problem, having in mind the heavy digital era we live in. Taking advantage of the Double Diamond process, the group first established the target audience, and later on, came to the conclusion that a digital multimedia adventure would be the best type of approach to gather all the attention possible. Hence MUTED is born, a first person experience of exploring and unraveling the truth behind the Huawei Scandal.

Inspirations

Seven Digital Deadly Sins

This interactive documentary produced by the NFB and The Guardian imagines what the seven deadly sins look like when updated for the digital age. Featuring “digital sin” confessions from a broad spectrum of contributors, Seven Digital Deadly Sins reveals that these celebrities use and abuse the internet as well.

Inside the Haiti Earthquake

Documentary game “Inside the Haiti Earthquake” invites web visitors to learn about disaster relief through the perspectives of journalists, aid workers, and survivors.
